How it works

  
Each closed contour in the image is treated as a path in the complex plane, z(t) = x(t) + i·y(t), traversed once as t runs from 0 to 1. Any such loop can be written as a sum of rotating vectors (epicycles):

  
z(t) = Σₖ cₖ · e^(2πik·t),   where   cₖ = ∫₀¹ z(t) · e^(−2πik·t) dt

  
The coefficients cₖ are computed here with the discrete Fourier transform of the traced boundary points. Truncating the sum at ±K terms gives an approximation that sharpens as K grows — the same bias–variance trade-off you meet in regression, applied to a curve instead of a scatterplot.

  
Pipeline: grayscale → Sobel edge detection (or background thresholding in outline mode) → contour extraction (marching squares) → uniform arc-length resampling → DFT per curve.
